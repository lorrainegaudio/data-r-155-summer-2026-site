Purpose

Subsetting is how analysts narrow a dataset to the rows and columns that actually matter for a question. In this guided activity, you will use select(), filter(), and the pipe %>% to build smaller, clearer datasets from larger ones while continuing to work in Quarto.

That work is worth doing now because nearly every later analysis depends on choosing the right variables, keeping the right cases, and writing steps that are easy to read and check. These skills will support later graphing, summarizing, modeling, and project-based analysis in R.

Chapter

  • Chapter_11_💈dplyrSubsetting.html
  • Focus on the Quarto setup, package loading, select(), filter(), and especially the section that combines filter() and select() in one pipeline. Pay close attention to how comparison operators and Boolean logic change which rows are kept, and why the chapter recommends filtering before selecting.

Data

No single external dataset required: built-in and package datasets used during the chapter.

This chapter uses mtcars plus dslabs datasets such as gapminder and us_contagious_diseases to practice row and column subsetting with realistic examples.

Guiding Questions

  • How does the chapter show the difference between using select() to choose columns and filter() to keep rows?
  • What does the pipe %>% make easier to read or understand when you work through a subsetting task?
  • Which comparison operators or Boolean rules in the chapter change which observations are kept, and how can you tell what each one is doing?
  • Why is it usually safer in this chapter to filter() first and select() second when you build a pipeline?
  • As you follow the examples, what checks help you confirm that your new subset contains the rows and columns you intended?

You do not submit answers to these questions, but use them to focus your reading, coding, and memo notes as you work through the Guided Activity.

Before You Move On

  • Run the examples in order so each subset depends on code that appears earlier in the file.
  • Check that your select(), filter(), and pipe examples return the rows and columns you expected.
  • Render or preview your Quarto document before leaving the activity.
