03.1.01 🛟 Help! Guided Activity

🌱 Core | 1 hr 30 min | Module 3

Purpose

Getting help is part of real R work, not a sign that you are doing something wrong.

In this guided activity, you will format an R Notebook with Markdown, render it to HTML, and practice a repeatable troubleshooting process by reading the Render tab, checking the first error, and using well-structured prompts to ask for targeted help when you get stuck.

That work is worth doing now because reproducible workflow depends on more than code that runs once in your Environment; it depends on a document that can build cleanly from top to bottom. These skills will help you solve errors more systematically, explain what went wrong, and keep your work readable for other people and for future-you.

Guided Activity

  • Time estimate: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Do: Monday
  • Open: Chapter_5_🛟Help!.html
  • Use: RStudio, an R Notebook, Markdown, the Preview/Knit tools in RStudio, and course-approved help resources for troubleshooting.
  • Have ready: Your course folder open in RStudio and a new R Notebook saved as chapter5_notes.Rmd so you can take notes, test changes, and knit as you work.
  • Produce: A chapter5_notes.Rmd file with a chapter title in YAML, a date field, organized Markdown headings, memo notes, and a successful HTML render of your notebook.

Chapter

  • Chapter_5_🛟Help!.html
  • Focus on the sections on R Notebook -> HTML Report, YAML Date Field, Markdown Headings, the difference between Preview and Knit, the Render Tab, and Structuring LLM Prompts.
  • Pay close attention to the chapter’s troubleshooting sequence: read the last error message, find the chunk or line that caused it, fix the first error, and knit again.

Data

No external dataset required.

This chapter is about document workflow, troubleshooting, and help-seeking rather than analyzing a dataset. You will work with an R Notebook, Markdown formatting, example knit errors, and structured troubleshooting prompts to learn how to build and fix a shareable HTML report.

Guiding Questions

  • What information belongs in the YAML header, and why does adding a date help make your notebook more reproducible?
  • How do Markdown headings change a notebook from a scratchpad into a document another person can follow?
  • What is the difference between Preview and Knit, and why can a notebook look fine in Preview but still fail when you knit it?
  • When knitting fails, what should you look at first in the Render tab, and why is fixing the first error usually the right move?
  • What details make a troubleshooting prompt more useful than saying “it won’t work,” and how can you ask an AI tool for help without outsourcing your thinking?

You do not submit answers to these questions, but use them to focus your reading, coding, and memo notes as you work through the Guided Activity.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Confirm that chapter5_notes.Rmd is saved in your course folder.
  • Confirm that your YAML header includes a clear title and date field.
  • Confirm that your notebook uses Markdown headings to organize the document.
  • Confirm that you understand the difference between Preview and Knit.
  • Confirm that you can read the Render tab and identify the first error when knitting fails.
  • Confirm that your notebook renders successfully to HTML.
  • Confirm that your memo notes describe troubleshooting clearly enough for someone else to follow.
