02.2.02 🧘 Human-Centered AI Reflective Discussion

Initial Post | 🌳 Advanced | 30 min | Module 2

Purpose

In this small-group discussion, you will practice making a defensible decision about whether AI use is appropriate in a realistic R learning situation.

You will create a short vignette about a student using AI, and then your group will use that vignette to evaluate whether the AI use supports learning or replaces the work that builds skills.

This discussion connects to the Human-Centered AI Guided Activity by asking you to apply the rule of thumb: attempt → document → ask → verify.

Discussion Preview

Important: This static page helps you prepare your discussion post. You cannot post to the discussion from this local site. When Canvas is available, post your response in your assigned discussion group.

Initial Post Directions

Your initial post is your original vignette from Task 3. Before you post, use Tasks 1 and 2 in the guided activity to think through what makes AI appropriate, inappropriate, or conditional in an R learning workflow.

  1. Open the discussion from Modules and click Reply.
  2. Include your opinions for Tasks 1 and 2 if the prompt asks for them.
  3. Write your own vignette from your experience, or invent one. Your vignette should describe a realistic situation where a student might use AI while learning R.
  4. Do not answer your own vignette in the initial post. Your classmates will evaluate it in the reply post.

Use This Template

Name: [Name of person in vignette]

Context: What task was [Name] doing?

Action: Provide a code snippet of 1–3 lines, or describe the action if not code.

Problem: State the error message, confusing output, or result.

Next Action: Did [Name] try something before asking for help?

AI Prompt: How does [Name] engage with AI?

A strong vignette is brief and specific. It gives your group enough information to decide whether the AI use is appropriate, not appropriate, or conditional.

Reply Post Directions

Your replies should evaluate your classmates’ vignettes. Reply to at least two classmates unless Canvas gives a different requirement.

  1. Read your classmate’s vignette carefully.
  2. Decide whether the AI use is appropriate, inappropriate, conditional, or unclear.
  3. Explain your reasoning using details from the vignette.
  4. Include a verification plan that would help the student check the AI output.

Use This Reply Structure

Evaluate: AI appropriate? Yes / No / Depends / Unsure

Reason: Explain your evaluation using details from the vignette.

Verification plan: How could [Name] check the AI output?

Minimum length: 3 to 6 complete sentences for each reply.

Good Reply Moves

  • Name whether the AI use is appropriate, not appropriate, conditional, or unclear.
  • Explain the condition or limit that matters most.
  • Refer to specific details from your classmate’s vignette.
  • Include a verification step, such as running the code, checking object names, comparing output to the chapter, or explaining the code in your own words.
  • Avoid short replies like “I agree” or “Nice job.” Those do not count as complete replies.
  • Your post should reflect your own thinking. AI may help with brainstorming, but it should not write the vignette or your evaluation for you.

How You Will Be Marked

This page is for the initial post. The reply post may be marked separately if it appears as a separate Canvas item.

✅ Complete

Your initial post is marked Complete when it:

  • is posted in your discussion group by the deadline,
  • uses the Task 3 vignette template,
  • describes a realistic AI-use situation related to learning R, coding, errors, reflection, or course workflow,
  • includes enough detail for a classmate to make an “AI appropriate?” decision, and
  • is brief, focused, and written in your own words.

🟡 Not Ready

Your initial post is marked Not Ready if it is:

  • too brief or too vague for a classmate to evaluate,
  • missing major parts of the vignette template,
  • only a general opinion about AI instead of a specific vignette, or
  • written as an answer to the vignette instead of as the vignette itself.

If your submission is marked 🟡 Not Ready, revise and resubmit before the assignment locks, if revision is available.

⭕ No Submission

No qualifying post was submitted, or the post was placed in the wrong location.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Make sure your vignette describes a specific situation, not just a general opinion about AI.
  • Check that your vignette includes enough detail for a classmate to evaluate it.
  • Do not answer your own vignette in the initial post.
  • Post in your assigned discussion group when Canvas is available.
