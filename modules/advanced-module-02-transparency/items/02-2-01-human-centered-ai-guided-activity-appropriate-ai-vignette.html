Purpose

In this 🌳 Advanced guided activity, you will read a short vignette about deciding when AI supports learning and when it replaces the work needed to build R coding skills.

This builds on your recent 🌱 Core work with R scripts, objects, error messages, and memoing because AI can either help you understand those steps or quietly take them away from you. In a realistic R workflow, the goal is not just to get code that runs; the goal is to understand, reproduce, and explain your work.

This reading prepares you to make better decisions about AI as a troubleshooting aid, concept tutor, translator, and verification tool.

Guided Activity

  • Time estimate: 30 minutes
  • Do: Wednesday
  • Open: Is-AI-Appropriate-Group-Discussion.html
  • Use: The reading and your recent experience working through R scripts, errors, objects, and memos.
  • Produce: Reading notes for yourself about when AI supports learning and when it becomes a shortcut around learning.

Reading

  • Is-AI-Appropriate-Group-Discussion.html
  • Read the Overview and Section 2: AI, Tenacity, and Human-Centered Learning.
  • Focus especially on the Tenacity-First AI rule, the AI help and harm examples, and the short vignettes about whether AI is appropriate.

Guiding Questions

  • What is the difference between using AI as help and using AI to avoid the learning work?
  • What information should you gather before asking AI for help with R code, an error message, or confusing output?
  • How can AI act as a translator, debugging coach, concept tutor, or practice generator without replacing your own thinking?
  • Which examples in the reading show responsible help-seeking, and which examples show cognitive delegation?
  • After AI gives a suggestion, what verification steps would help you decide whether the answer is correct and whether you actually understand it?

Use these questions to focus your reading. You do not submit separate answers to them on this page.
