02.2.03 🧘 Human-Centered AI Reflective Discussion

Reply Posts | 🌳 Advanced | 30 min | Module 2

Purpose

This assignment records credit for the reply step in the related Human-Centered AI Reflective Discussion. Your initial post asked you to share your thinking about Tasks 1–2 and create an original Task 3 vignette about AI use in an R learning situation.

For this reply post, you will read classmates’ posts and respond briefly to two peers. You will comment on their ideas for Tasks 1–2 and complete Task 4 by evaluating each classmate’s vignette.

This page exists so the initial post and reply work can be evaluated separately.

Discussion Preview

Important: This static page helps you prepare your replies. You cannot post to the discussion from this local site. When Canvas is available, post your replies in your assigned discussion group.

At a Glance

  • Time estimate: 30 minutes
  • Due: Saturday
  • Purpose of this page: This is how you get credit for the reply step.
  • Complete your replies in: Your Human-Centered AI Reflective Discussion group
  • What you will do: Reply to at least two classmates in your discussion group.

Open the discussion from Modules so Canvas takes you into your group’s copy of the discussion.

Complete Your Replies

  1. Open the Human-Centered AI Reflective Discussion from Modules.
  2. Read classmates’ posts in your discussion group.
  3. Choose at least two classmates’ posts to evaluate.
  4. Briefly respond to one idea from each classmate’s Tasks 1–2 comments.
  5. Use Task 4 to evaluate each classmate’s Task 3 vignette.

Required Reply Structure

Each reply should include both parts below.

Part 1: Respond Briefly to Tasks 1–2

  • Name one idea from your classmate’s Tasks 1–2 comments that stood out to you.
  • Briefly explain whether you agree, disagree, or would add a condition or limit.

Part 2: Complete Task 4 for Their Vignette

Evaluate: AI appropriate? Yes / No / Depends / Unsure

Reason: Explain your evaluation.

Verification plan: How could [Name] check the AI output?

Your evaluation should use details from the classmate’s vignette, not just general opinions about AI.

Good Reply Moves

  • Name whether the AI use is appropriate, not appropriate, conditional, or unclear.
  • Explain the condition or limit that matters most.
  • Point to a specific detail from the classmate’s vignette.
  • Include a practical verification step, such as running the code, checking object names, comparing output to the chapter, or explaining the code in plain language.
  • Keep the reply focused on helping the classmate think more clearly about responsible AI use.

Weak Reply Moves

  • Only saying “I agree,” “same,” or “nice job.”
  • Giving a general opinion about AI without evaluating the vignette.
  • Skipping the verification plan.
  • Responding to only one classmate when two replies are required.

How You Will Be Marked

✅ Complete

Your reply work is marked Complete if your discussion replies:

  • are posted in the correct discussion group thread,
  • respond to at least two classmates,
  • briefly respond to one idea from each classmate’s Tasks 1–2 comments,
  • evaluate each classmate’s Task 3 vignette using the Task 4 structure,
  • include a clear reason and a practical verification plan, and
  • are specific enough to count as meaningful participation.

🟡 Not Ready

Your reply work is marked Not Ready if it:

  • is missing or posted in the wrong place,
  • responds to only one classmate,
  • does not respond to classmates’ vignettes,
  • does not include an evaluation, reason, and verification plan,
  • is too brief or too vague, or
  • only gives general opinions about AI without using details from classmates’ posts.

If your work is marked 🟡 Not Ready, revise your discussion replies before the assignment locks, if revision is available.

⭕ No Submission

No qualifying replies were posted.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Check that you replied to at least two classmates.
  • Check that each reply includes Part 1 and Part 2.
  • Make sure each reply includes a verification plan.
  • Post your replies in your assigned Canvas discussion group when Canvas is available.
