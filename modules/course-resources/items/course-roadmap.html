Choose Your Course Path

All students must earn ✅ Complete on all 🌱 Core items to receive a C or better in this course. Your bundle path depends on how many 🌳 Advanced activities you choose to complete in addition to Core.

Use the chart below to compare the workload, level of challenge, and weekly time commitment so you can choose a path that fits your goals and your schedule.

You do not need to decide which Advanced activities you will do today. Explore the topics to see what interests you. Start with the most realistic plan for the time and effort you can actually sustain, then adjust later if needed.

Important: You will complete all 🌱 Core modules, regardless of which bundle you choose. The bundle chart helps you decide how much additional 🌳 Advanced work you want to take on.

Which Advanced activities count toward ✅ Complete? Only activities listed as module requirements count toward the Advanced completion total. These are usually interactive or graded items, such as a discussion post or reply, a quiz, an assignment submission, or a revision. Guided Activity pages help you prepare for Advanced work, but they do not count toward the Advanced total by themselves.

Weekly Rhythm

This course follows a steady weekly rhythm, so you can plan ahead. In most weeks, 🌱 Core work takes about 5 hours. If you are aiming for the B path, plan for about 1.5 more hours of 🌳 Advanced work. If you are aiming for the A path, plan for about 2 more hours of 🌳 Advanced work.

In most modules, Core comes first. Advanced work is added after you have made progress on the related Core items. Wednesdays and Thursdays may require slightly more time because of revision deadlines. You can work ahead in many situations and submit early to prevent a mid-week bottleneck.

For example, you may choose to start your course week on Sunday and work one day ahead of the due dates. You may also choose to invest more time in Advanced work one week than another, depending on your interests and schedule.

  • Core rhythm: Each week includes two Core chapter sequences. Each sequence usually includes a guided activity, a Practice Space, and a checked submission or file submission.
  • Advanced rhythm: Advanced work is more flexible. You choose the Advanced activities you want and can realistically keep up with that week.
  • Revision rhythm: If a Core item earns 🟡 Not Ready, use the revision window. Do not wait until the end of the week to check your status.

Important: Advanced items have deadlines with no token options. Opening a page is not the same as completing the requirement. Check Canvas Module Trackers for ✅ Complete on the items that count.

Unlocking Advanced Modules

Think of 🌱 Core as the main trail through the course. Each time you earn ✅ Complete on a Core module, you unlock the next set of 🌳 Advanced options. These Advanced modules are where you get more choice: you can pick the activities that interest you, match your goals, or give you the kind of challenge you want that week.

The course starts with both 🌱 Core Module 1 and 🌳 Advanced Module 1 open. After that, completing each Core module opens the next Advanced module. This keeps Advanced work connected to the Core skills you have already built.

What this means for planning: If an Advanced module remains locked, your next step is simple: complete the Core module that unlocks it. Once that Core checkpoint earns ✅ Complete, a new Advanced set becomes available.

Reminder: Unlocking an Advanced module gives you access to more choices, but it does not remove the deadlines. Advanced activities still have their own due dates, and there are no token options for Advanced work.
