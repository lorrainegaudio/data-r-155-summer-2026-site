01.2.01 🐣 Resilient Beginnings Guided Activity: Why I'm Learning R

🌳 Advanced | 45 min | Module 1

Home Course Calendar Gradebook Book a Meeting Discussion Groups

Purpose

This guided activity helps you make an early plan for why you are learning R, what you want from this course, and which course path best fits that goal right now.

Building on your first Core work with RStudio, scripts, and beginning workflow habits, you will review course materials, then do private freewriting to think through your goals, likely constraints, and what kind of effort you can actually sustain.

This matters in real-world R workflows because clear goals, realistic planning, and honest decisions about effort make it easier to stay on track as the course becomes more demanding.

Plan Your Course Path

  • Time estimate: about 30 minutes
  • Have ready: your course syllabus, module overview materials, and a realistic sense of your time, goals, and outside commitments

Part A: Explore Why R and How the Course Works

Guiding Questions

  • Why am I taking this course now?
  • Where might I actually use R in the next 1–3 years?
  • Does learning R matter a lot, somewhat, or only a little for my current goals? Why?
  • What would count as “enough” learning for me in this course?
  • Which bundle path seems to fit that goal right now?
  • What might make that plan hard to follow through on?
  • What would I need to do in the first two weeks to make my plan realistic?

You do not submit separate answers to these questions. Use them to focus your reading and thinking as you work through this part.

Part B: Private Reflection Freewrite

This part is for private planning notes. You will not submit this writing for review. The goal is to think honestly about what you want from this course, what may get in the way, and what actions would make your plan realistic.

You do not need to share every private detail later in the discussion. Use this space to think honestly first, then decide what you want to share publicly with your peers.

What to Write

  • Write short notes for yourself in response to the guiding questions from Part A.
  • Focus on your reasons for taking the course, where R might matter for your goals, and what level of learning would be enough right now.
  • Decide which bundle path best fits your current goals and available effort.
  • Name any constraints that may interfere with success, and write down what you will do in the first two weeks to make your plan realistic.
  • End your notes with these two sentences:
    • Right now, the bundle path that seems to fit me best is ______ because ______.
    • This first week, I will ______ to make this plan realistic.

Keep in Mind

  • This reflection is for planning, not for proving that you already know exactly what your future will be.
  • Be practical. A realistic plan is better than an ambitious plan that you cannot sustain.
  • Your notes here will help you prepare for the later small-group discussion, where you will share what you discovered about your goals and course path.
  • Your current plan is a starting point, not a contract. You may revise it later as you learn more about the course and your own needs.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Review the course path and bundle information.
  • Watch the required “Why R?” video.
  • Use the optional videos only if you want another perspective on R, Python, or language choice.
  • Complete your private freewrite notes.
  • Keep your private notes available so you can prepare for the later reflective discussion.
