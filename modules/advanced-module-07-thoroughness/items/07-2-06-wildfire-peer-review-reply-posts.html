Purpose

This assignment records credit for replying to your peers in the related 🌳 Discussion Group activity. Your initial post is evaluated on the discussion page. This page is the reminder and assignment checkpoint for the reply step.

Return to 🔥 Wildfire Discussion: Peer Review and post your replies there. This page exists so the initial post and the reply work can be evaluated separately.

At a Glance

  • Time estimate: 20 to 30 minutes
  • Due: Friday, Week 7
  • Purpose of this page: This is how you get credit for the reply step.
  • Complete your replies in: 🔥 Wildfire Discussion: Peer Review
  • What you will do: Reply to two classmates in your discussion group. You are encouraged to continue the conversation after meeting this minimum when it helps support your group’s learning.

Complete Your Replies

  1. Open 🔥 Wildfire Discussion: Peer Review.
  2. Read your classmates’ posts in your discussion group.
  3. Reply to two classmates in your discussion group.
  4. Make sure each reply responds to what the classmate actually wrote or asked about.
  5. Post your replies in the discussion.
  6. If the conversation continues in a useful way, you are encouraged to return and keep participating beyond the required two replies to support each other’s learning.
  7. Return to this assignment page and complete the Canvas submission step shown here, if required.

What Strong Replies Include

  • a reference to something specific from your peer’s post or attached draft
  • a useful response to their question about code, simulation logic, memo writing, file formatting, or rendering
  • one added thought, clarification, debugging idea, follow-up question, or revision suggestion
  • more than simple agreement or praise

Good replies help move the conversation forward. They do not just say “I agree” or “nice job.” The required minimum is two replies, but thoughtful follow-up after that is encouraged when it helps your group ask better questions, clarify ideas, troubleshoot problems, and support each other’s learning.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Make sure your replies are actually posted in the discussion thread.
  • Check that each reply is specific and substantial enough to count.
  • Make sure you replied to two classmates, not just one.
  • Return here after posting your discussion replies.
  • If this assignment page requires a submission action in Canvas, complete that step before the deadline.

The minimum requirement is two replies. You are encouraged to continue the conversation after that when it helps your group clarify ideas, troubleshoot problems, and support each other’s learning.

How You Will Be Marked

✅ Complete if your discussion replies:

  • are posted in the correct discussion group thread
  • include two meaningful replies
  • respond specifically to what classmates actually posted
  • help continue the discussion in a useful way

🟡 Not Ready if your replies:

  • are missing or posted in the wrong place
  • include fewer than two qualifying replies
  • are too brief or too vague
  • do not meet the required reply expectations for the discussion

⭕ No Submission if no qualifying replies were posted.

If your work is marked 🟡 Not Ready, revise your discussion replies before the assignment locks, if allowed.
