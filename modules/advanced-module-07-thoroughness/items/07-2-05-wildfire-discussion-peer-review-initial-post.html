🌳 Advanced Module 7 | Thoroughness

Purpose

This discussion is a place to get useful help with any part of the Wildfire activities, including Parts A, B, or C. You will share a draft file that your group can actually open, explain where you are stuck or uncertain, and ask one or two specific questions so peers can respond with targeted help on code, simulation logic, memo writing, file formatting, or rendering.

This peer review step matters because it gives you a chance to improve your wildfire report before the final submission. Good peer review is specific, practical, and focused on helping a classmate make the next revision move.

At a Glance

  • Time estimate: 30 minutes
  • Initial post due: Thursday, Week 7
  • Reply due: Friday, Week 7
  • Post in: 🔥 Wildfire Discussion
  • You will submit:
    • a wildfire draft attachment
    • a short context note
    • one or two specific help questions
    • replies to at least 2 peers

Open the discussion from Modules so Canvas takes you into your discussion-group copy of the discussion.

Before You Post

  1. Complete as much of Parts A, B, and C as you can before posting.
  2. Render your file, if possible, so peers can more easily see what you built.
  3. Check that the file you plan to attach opens correctly and matches your current draft.
  4. If this is your first discussion-group activity, make sure you have joined a discussion group before posting.

Related Activity or File

  • Complete first: your wildfire draft work from Parts A, B, and/or C
  • Have ready: your current wildfire draft, such as Fire-Assignment-PartX-StudentTemplate.html if it rendered successfully, or Fire-Assignment-PartX-StudentTemplate.qmd if it did not

Initial Post Directions

Post your current wildfire draft and ask for help on a specific part of the work. You may ask for help on anything in Parts A, B, or C, including code, object creation, simulation logic, memo writing, file formatting, or rendering problems. The more concrete your question is, the more useful your peer feedback will be.

  1. Open the discussion from Modules and click Reply.
  2. Attach your draft:
    • Preferred: Fire-Assignment-PartX-StudentTemplate.html
    • If your file did not render: upload Fire-Assignment-PartX-StudentTemplate.qmd instead
    • Important: Your group members need to be able to open what you built. If you upload a file that cannot be opened, your post may be marked Not Ready.
  3. Add a short context note of 2 to 4 sentences:
    • what you were trying to do
    • which parts or tasks you completed
    • where you are uncertain or stuck
  4. Ask one or two specific questions for help:
    • Ask a concrete question your group can respond to.
    • Good examples:
      • Why does my replicate() block return the wrong shape?
      • Does my histogram for Task 10 actually support my probability estimate?
      • Am I using meanlog[idx] and sdlog[idx] correctly in Task 6?
      • Why is my QMD file not rendering even though the code chunk runs?
      • Does my memo explain the simulation results clearly enough?
    • Do not write vague questions such as “Any feedback?” or “Does this look okay?”

You may ask for help on any part of the wildfire report.

Reply Post Directions

Your replies should help a peer move forward. Good replies answer the question the writer actually asked, point to something specific in the file or explanation, and offer one practical next step. You must reply to at least 2 peers.

  1. Reply to at least 2 classmates in your discussion group.
  2. If possible, choose posts that do not already have many replies so more people get help.
  3. Your reply should include all of the following:
    • a reference to something specific from the peer’s file, draft, screenshot, or explanation
    • a direct answer to the peer’s help question
    • one useful suggestion, clarification, debugging step, formatting fix, or revision idea
  4. Minimum length: 4 to 8 sentences per reply

Good Peer Review Moves

  • Point to one part of the work that is already clear and effective.
  • Identify one place where the code, explanation, memo, file formatting, or render output could be clearer or stronger.
  • Answer the writer’s question directly instead of giving generic praise.
  • Suggest one next step the writer can actually try.
  • Keep feedback respectful, specific, and focused on improving the work.

Discussion Guidelines

  • Post on time so someone in your group can actually review your work.
  • Attach the correct file and make sure it opens.
  • Ask one or two specific help questions.
  • Give feedback that uses evidence from your peer’s actual post or file.
  • Help with code, memos, file formatting, and render problems when relevant.
  • Keep replies respectful, useful, and focused on revision.
  • Follow the AI directions shown on the assignment page. Your post should still reflect your own thinking and coursework.

How You Will Be Marked

✅ Complete when all are true:

  • You posted your wildfire draft with a viewable attachment.
  • You included a short context note.
  • You asked at least one specific question for help.
  • If you used AI, you described how you used it and explained the code or idea in your own words.
  • You replied to at least 2 peers.
  • Your replies directly answered the peer’s question and provided useful, specific feedback.

🟡 Not Ready if any of these are missing:

  • No attachment, wrong artifact, or file cannot be opened.
  • No clear context note.
  • Only vague questions, such as “Any feedback?”
  • No replies to peers, or fewer than 2 replies.
  • Replies are too brief, vague, or not based on the peer’s actual draft.
  • AI-generated code or explanations are included without clarifying how AI was used or what the code does.

⭕ No Submission

If your submission is marked 🟡 Not Ready, revise your post and resubmit before the discussion locks.

Note: This discussion may help you strengthen your report before Final Revision, but it is not required for the 🌱 Core Signature Assignment.
