Purpose

Before you can learn R well, you need a working setup on your own computer and a clear sense of why this course uses R and RStudio Desktop.

In this guided activity, you will learn what R and RStudio each do, install both in the correct order, and review the course expectations around reproducibility, memoing, and responsible AI use.

That work is worth doing now because every later chapter depends on having the right software installed locally and understanding the workflow standards the course will use. This setup gives you a clean starting point for writing, running, saving, and explaining your work in R.

Guided Activity

  • Time estimate: 30 min
  • Do: Monday
  • Open: Preface.html
  • Use: Your personal computer, internet access, the CRAN download site for R, and the Posit download page for RStudio Desktop.
  • Have ready: A computer you can install software on, enough storage space for both programs, and your operating system identified so you can choose the correct installer.
  • Produce: A working local installation of both R and RStudio Desktop and the ability to open RStudio on your computer.

Chapter

  • Preface.html
  • Focus on Why Learn R?, Acquiring the Software, Download and Install R, Download and Install RStudio, and the chapter terms for R, RStudio Desktop, and LLMs.
  • Pay close attention to the requirement to install R first and RStudio Desktop second, and to the expectation that coursework happens locally in the desktop version of RStudio.

Data

No dataset required.

This guided activity is about getting your working environment ready for the course. Instead of analyzing data, you will use installation instructions, software download pages, and short video tutorials to set up R and RStudio Desktop correctly.

Guiding Questions

  • What is the difference between R and RStudio Desktop, and why do you need both?
  • Why does the preface insist on using the desktop version of RStudio instead of browser-based tools for this course?
  • What could go wrong if you install RStudio before R, or if RStudio cannot detect your R installation?
  • After installation, what simple check tells you that your setup is ready for the rest of the course?
  • How does the preface connect the software setup to broader course expectations, such as reproducibility, memoing, and responsible AI use?

You do not submit answers to these questions, but use them to focus your reading, coding, and memo notes as you work through the Guided Activity.

Before You Leave This Page

  • Confirm that R is installed.
  • Confirm that RStudio Desktop is installed.
  • Open RStudio Desktop successfully.
  • Make a note of any setup issue you still need help with.
